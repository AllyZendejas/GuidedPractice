Education
The first step in beoming a PA is attending a 4 year university and gettign your bachelors degree. There are no restrictions on choosing your major, but you will need to meet all the pre-requisetes for the program you want to attend. A biology or healthare major will have the majority of pre-requisets you will need to apply to PA school. After you reieve your bachelors degree you will be able to go to PA school. Typically this is 24 month program with the first year being diactic, learning in a classroom, and the second being clinical, working in a hospital. PA school is mentally and academically chalenging beause you are learning about many diffrent specialties and healthcare as a whole at an expedited rate. After you graduate from PA school you will take the PANCE, and once you pass this exam you will be able you will earn your certification and be able to work as a PA. The top PA schools in the United states are Duke University, University of Iowa, and Baylor university.                   
One of the great things about PA school is the amount of choices you have after getting certified, and how you can change specielties later on. Some of the more popular specialties for PA-Cs are Family Medicine, Dermatology, and General surgery.  
The salary of a PA depends greatly on the specialty they work in but the average is $108,000. The highest paying specialty is cardiovascular surgery which averages at $137,000. The lower paying specialtys are pediatrics and Primary care.
The PA profession was created in 1965 as a way to provide high quality health care to those who live in lower income distrits. The first class of PAs was taught at Duke university. PAs attend school for a shorter period becuase it is required for the students to have previous medical experience such as working as a CNA, EMT, or Pharmacy Tech.
In 2021 the PA proffesion was named the #1 on the top 100 jobs list accoding to US News and World Reports. Many PAs say they are extremly happy to be a PA because they can help contribute to their community, while also living a normal life and not going into a crazy amount of debt. Most PAs work an average of 40 hours, wheras a dotor typically works upwards of 60 due to having to be on-call.
